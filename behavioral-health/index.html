THE CHALLENGE

The Logistical Burden Holding Back MAT Programs

Daily clinic visits for Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) burden patients, compounded by the stigma around opioid use disorder.

SureAdhere gives clinics flexible, virtual therapy options. Patients stay committed while providers track adherence through continuous feedback, for better outcomes with greater convenience and dignity.

PLATFORM CAPABILITIES

Three ways SureAdhere transforms MAT.

Improve Treatment Access

Expand access to high-quality MAT for underserved communities and people facing barriers to in-person care.

  • Virtual therapy for patients without reliable transportation
  • Serve patients across urban and rural settings
  • Higher provider-to-patient ratios reduce wait times
  • Compatible with existing clinic workflows and EHR systems

Reduce Treatment Burden

Remove the time, cost, and travel burdens of in-person MAT with secure remote treatment support.

  • Patients record doses via video from home, no clinic commute
  • Saves patients an average of 5.5 hours of travel per week
  • Reduces median weekly travel costs by $72
  • Real-time alerts for providers when a dose is missed

Increase Autonomy

Help patients take charge of recovery with a platform that fits their lives, flexible and convenient throughout treatment.

  • Flexible dosing windows that adapt to patients' schedules
  • Secure in-app messaging for discreet, stigma-free communication
  • Treatment calendar and progress tracking for self-monitoring
  • Multilingual and offline-capable for diverse patient needs

Vermont's Virtual MAT Program: Reduces Costs and Boosts Retention.

Accessing Medication-Assisted Treatment (MAT) in Vermont comes with significant challenges. Nearly half of patients enrolled in outpatient MAT programs disengage from treatment within their first year due to the heavy logistical burden. The Howard Center's innovative VDOT program, combining a secure medication dispenser with SureAdhere, transformed those outcomes.
